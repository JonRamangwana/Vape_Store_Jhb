Lifespan of a vape depends on different factors that include type of vape, juice, frequency of vaping, pod size, battery, maintenance, etc. Let’s get deep with the factors mentioned in this article.

1. Type of vape
There are disposable & non disposable vapes. Disposable vapes are thrown away once battery is empty & juice gets finished. They come with specifications to specify the number of puffs. When we are speaking statistics a disposable vape has a median of 600 puffs until it reaches the end of its road. Non disposable vapes are for keeps. Battery is rechargeable & there are tanks to refill juice.

2. Juice
Strong juice with a high concentration of nicotine, THC, or any other active ingredient can last long. Take a few puffs of strong juice & get in the vibe for a long time. Other types of juice are thick, this makes them last longer. They vaporize slow because they are less volatile.

3. Frequency of vaping
Somebody who smokes more often can shorten lifespan of a vape. Smoking more often is highly recommended by the way. Vaping once a day can make one pod fill last more than one week. While smoking each hour can empty a pod one day.

4. Pod size
A big tank carries more juice, more juice last longer than less juice.

5. Battery
When the battery is full, vape can go up to a week for an average smoker. A battery with a big capacity can carry more charge meaning a long time of smoking before it gets empty.

6. Maintenance
A vape that’s maintained properly can last longer. When coils & pods are constantly cleaned it improves atomization efficiency. Experienced vapers might understand priming. There is a process called priming. When a new vape is bought fill pod & leave vape for a few minutes to allow wick to absorb juice before taking first puff. Wet wick, no dry hits, longer lifespan. .
